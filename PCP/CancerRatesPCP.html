Cancer Rates in Iowa: Parallel Coordinates Plot

The map you're looking at compares the age adjusted rate of cancer incidence from 2016 - 2020 in a multivariate manner. Cancer's chosen for comparison are Prostate, Non-Hodgkins Lymphoma (NHL), Colorectal, and Lung. These were chosen because they are high in Iowa and could be associated with agriculure. This allows for users to investigate which types of cancer are common in different regions of Iowa, and also identify trends. Agriculture is not included in this visualization because I wanted to be able to compare cancer rates before looking at the influence of agriculure.

  • P.C. enables you (the user) an overview of all the data.
  • There are three primary points of interaction; brushing P.C. axes, the dropdown attribute selection, and the map hovering. Each of them aid in assisting the user to get the data they need by zooming and filtering the data.
  • The map tooltip interaction allows precise information, thus serving as a proxy for details on demand.

Different trends can be taken from this visualization. Both Colorectal and Prostate incidence rate are high in Northwestern Iowa, while Lung incidence rate is high in the South and Southeast. In addition to these hotspots, for all cancers considered, there are hotspots surrounidng Des Moines and Cedar Rapids. This is likely due to the population density in these areas.

Source:
