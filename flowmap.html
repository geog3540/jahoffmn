Internal Migration in Brazil Flow Map

Download Flow Map

I chose specific symbology to communicate how people are moving throughout Brazil. Only the top 50 flows are shown to reduce confusion and overlapping on the map. I chose to use monotone tapered half arrows to display magnitude without having to check the legend on the map. I chose a divergent color scheme to match the color scheme of the nodes. I chose to break migration flows into 4 quantiles to best describe flows of different magnitudes. For nodes, I chose to do manual breaks based on the total net migration of each node. Natural breaks and quantiles both I chose to have a divergent color scheme showing states with negative net migration in red, and states with positive net migration in blue. I wish I could change the node size to be divergent, however I could not find a way to do that on FlowMapper. The largest flows on this map seem to be all towards Sao Paulo, as it is receiving the largest amount of net migration in Brazil from this data. The general direction of flow seems to be towards more south-central Brazil. Interestingly, not many people are migrating to and from the northwest of Brazil, due to the low amount of population there. I also thought it was interesting that Rio de Janeiro has strong negative net migration, mostly to other surrounding states.
