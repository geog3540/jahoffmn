Animal Agriculture and Cancer Rates a Self Organizing Map

The tool SOMVIS was used to visualize the multivariate relationships between animal agriculure and cancer rates in Iowa. SOMVIS clusters the data into groups that are similar across all variables considered. Overall, the similiarity in color of each zip code indicates more similar clustering. Three different visualizations are produced using SOMVIS. There is a choropleth map where counties are colored based on the cluster they blong to, a parallel coordinates plot which shows the values of each variable for each cluster, and self organizing map. The self organizing map plots each cluster as circle which is scaled by the number of zip codes in that cluster. The hexagons between each cluster are colored based on how close or apart the clusers are, where dark gray or black rrepresent dissimilarity between neighboring clusters, and light gray or white represent similarity. Below is a visualization of all variables considered, which are listed below.

Variables used for the SOMVIS Visualization:

  • Normalized percentages of cattle (Pct_Cattle), swine (Pct_Swine), and chicken (Pct_Chicken) created by dividing the amount of each respective animal in the chosen zip by the total of that animal in the state.
  • Age adjusted rate of cancer incidence from 2016 - 2020 for Prostate, Non-Hodgkins Lymphoma (NHL), Colorectal, and Lung.

Cattle and Cancer Incidence
By removing swine and chicken from the analysis, the relationship between cattle and cancer incidence can be seen. The most obvious clusters are those in green shades, mostly in Northwest Iowa which have both high cattle and high incidence rates for Colorectal and Prostate.

Swine and Cancer Incidence
By removing cattle and chicken from the analysis, the relationship between swine and cancer incidence can be seen. Less obvious clusters are seen, however, Northwest Iowa is still highly clustered. As seen in the point symbol map, swine is highly distriubted across the state, with a slightly higher concentration in the Northwest. Due to this, clusters are much less distinct.

Chicken and Cancer Incidence
